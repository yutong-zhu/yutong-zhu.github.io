Cell-Penetrating Peptides (CPPs) Based Zinc Finger Delivery System
Aaron Rosenstein, Yutong Zhu, Michael Garton
In progress

It is theoretically proven that CPP-based delivery system can achieve stable transfection with the gene of interest. This work focuses on designing and evaluating different CPPs' effectiveness with the zinc finger construct, which will be validated through the utilization of the previously constructued landing pad cell lines. This process involves Gibson assembly, protein purification and transfection.

Design of Landing Pad Cell Lines Targeting Specific Safe Harbour Sites for Efficient Gene Editing
Aaron Rosenstein, Yutong Zhu, Michael Garton
Final Presentation Slides

This work involves the design and construction of a library of landing pad cell lines targeting pSH231 and AAVS1 safe harbour sites. The library provides an efficient approach for recombinase mediated cassette exchange (RMCE) with a specific promoter and gene of interest. Professional cell biology techniques such as molecular cloning, PCR/qPCR, Gibson assembly and CRISPR-Cas9 transfection were utilized. The software Benchling was also used to design primers and select restriction enzymes for PCR/digestion simulations in advance of performing experiments.

Design of a High-Throughput TRACER Platform Compatible with 96-Well Plates
Nancy T. Li, Ruonan Cao, Yutong Zhu, Alison P. McGuigan
To be submitted, 2021.
Final Report / Final Presentation Slides

This work contains the design of a high-throughput TRACER platform that is compatible with the 96 well-plate format through the stacking of 6 layers of scaffolds with regions blocked off by PMMA. The experience has improved my 3D designs skills through the utilization of CAD, G-code and bioprinters. In addition to that, I have also gained new skills such as live cell imaging with fluorescence microscopy and image processing via ImageJ.

Final Report / Final Presentation Slides

Effects of Drugs on Cancer Cell Metastasis
BME346 Biomedical Engineering & Omics Technologies, University of Toronto
Final Presentation Slides / Project Proposal

This project focused on the combined effects of drugs on cancer metastasis. Our team discovered great potential in the combination of platelet inhibition and natural killer (NK) cell enhancement, where platelet inhibition sensitizes tumor cells to NK cell activity and NK enhancement increases cytotoxic effects.

“HERON” Biological Payload Design
University of Toronto Aerospace Team
Video

The payload subteam of the UTAT Space Systems division specialized in assessing the risk of infection during long term space missions. The objectives were to engineer C. albicans to express GFP with specific genes and create a statistical method for quantifying this gene expression.

UT BIOME Prosthetic Leg
University of Toronto Biomedical Engineering Design Team

This project aimed to design and manufacture a fully functioning prosthetic leg targeted towards pediatric patients. Initial design ideas were generated through AutoCAD & SolidWorks, with the finalized version of the prototype selected by the client.

Traffic Cone Deployment Robot
AER201 Engineering Design, University of Toronto
Final Report / Video

This project focused on the design and fabrication of a fully autonomous mobile robot that can deploy miniature traffic cones based on the detection of "cracks" and "holes". The robot was constructed with omnidirectional mecanum wheels, infrared sensors, as well as an interactive LCD and keyboard user interface.
