Additionally, I was previously a Research Assistant at Garton Lab focusing on the subject of Synthetic Biology with the guidance of Michael Garton for a period of 8 months. 3D reconstruction and multi-organ mapping of the female mouse reproductive system as a function of age
Yutong Zhu, Mia Grahn, Aanya Kheterpal, Ashleigh Crawford, Denis Wirtz, Ashley Kiemen
Poster Presentation

This study involves construction of 3D, multi-organ volumes of the normal reproductive system of mouse at different age groups using CODA, a novel machine-learning pipeline for 3D image analysis, to identify major changes to ovary, fallopian, cervix and vagina morphology and immune infiltration with age.

Three-dimensional genomic mapping of human pancreatic tissue reveals striking multifocality and genetic heterogeneity in precancerous lesions
Alicia M Braxton+, Ashley L Kiemen+, Mia P Grahn, André Forjaz, Jeeun Parksong, Jaanvi Mahesh Babu, Jiaying Lai, Lily Zheng, Noushin Niknafs, Liping Jiang, Haixia Cheng, Qianqian Song, Rebecca Reichel, Sarah Graham, Alexander I Damanakis, Catherine G Fischer, Stephanie Mou, Cameron Metz, Julie Granger, Xiao-Ding Liu, Niklas Bachmann, Yutong Zhu, YunZhou Liu, Cristina Almagro-Pérez, Ann Chenyu Jiang, Jeonghyun Yoo, Bridgette Kim, Scott Du, Eli Foster, Jocelyn Y Hsu, Paula Andreu Rivera, Linda C Chu, Fengze Liu, Elliot K Fishman, Alan Yuille, Nicholas J Roberts, Elizabeth D Thompson, Robert B Scharpf, Toby C Cornish, Yuchen Jiao+, Rachel Karchin, Ralph H Hruban, Pei-Hsun Wu, Denis Wirtz+, Laura D Wood+
Nature (Submitted), 2023
bioRxiv Preprint

This study involves quantitative assessment of human PanINs using CODA, a novel machine-learning pipeline for 3D image analysis that generates quantifiable models of large pieces of human pancreas with single-cell resolution. In this study, by leveraging the novel 3D genomic mapping approach CODA, for the first time, we are able to describe the spatial and genetic multifocality of human PanINs, providing important insights into the initiation and progression of pancreatic neoplasia.

Characterization of post-translational modification (PTM) of freshwater quagga mussel adhesive protein
Yutong Zhu, Angelico Obille, Eli Sone
Undergraduate Thesis

This study involves the investigation of adhesive protein mechanisms through PTMs of freshwater quagga mussel proteins. Through a coherent understanding and characterization of adhesive proteins, this work provides a basis for the development of bioadhesives. Underwater adhesion mechanism utilized in mussel attachment also provides a well-established role model for developing novel bioadhesives such as self-healing materials for would closure and advanced coatings in aqueous environment.

Cell-penetrating peptides (CPPs) based zinc finger delivery system
Yutong Zhu, Aaron Rosenstein, Michael Garton

It is theoretically proven that CPP-based delivery system can achieve stable transfection with the gene of interest. This work focuses on designing and evaluating different CPPs' effectiveness with the zinc finger construct, which will be validated through the utilization of the previously constructued landing pad cell lines.

Design of landing pad cell lines targeting specific safe harbour sites for efficient gene editing
Yutong Zhu, Aaron Rosenstein, Michael Garton

This study involves the design and construction of a library of landing pad cell lines targeting pSH231 and AAVS1 safe harbour sites. The library provides an efficient approach for recombinase mediated cassette exchange (RMCE) with a specific promoter and gene of interest. Professional cell biology techniques such as molecular cloning, PCR/qPCR, Gibson assembly and CRISPR-Cas9 transfection were utilized. The software Benchling was also used to design primers and select restriction enzymes for PCR/digestion simulations in advance of performing experiments.

An off-the-shelf multi-well scaffold-supported platform for tumour organoid-based tissues
Nancy T. Li, Nila C. Wu, Ruonan Cao, Jose L. Cadavid, Simon Latour, Xiaoya Lu, Yutong Zhu, Mirjana Mijalkovic, Reza Roozitalab, Natalie Landon-Brace, Faiyaz Notta, Alison P. McGuigan
Biomaterials, 2022

In this study, we developed the Scaffold-supported Platform for Organoid-based Tissues (SPOT) platform, a 3D gel-embedded in vitro platform that can be produced in a 96- or 384-well plate format and enables the generation of flat, thin, and dimensionally-defined microgels. We envision 96/384-SPOT will provide a useful tool to assess drug sensitivity of patient-derived organoids and easily integrate into the drug discovery pipeline.

Effects of Drugs on Cancer Cell Metastasis
BME346 Biomedical Engineering & Omics Technologies, University of Toronto
Final Presentation Slides / Project Proposal

This project focused on the combined effects of drugs on cancer metastasis. Our team discovered great potential in the combination of platelet inhibition and natural killer (NK) cell enhancement, where platelet inhibition sensitizes tumor cells to NK cell activity and NK enhancement increases cytotoxic effects.

Video

The payload subteam of the UTAT Space Systems division specialized in assessing the risk of infection during long term space missions. The objectives were to engineer C. albicans to express GFP with specific genes and create a statistical method for quantifying this gene expression.
